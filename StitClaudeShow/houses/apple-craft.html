01 · Palette

Cool grays, blue-leaning. One confident accent.

Apple-Craft leans blue-gray rather than warm. Shadows are soft, long, and never pure black. System blue is the default accent unless the brand demands otherwise — and green is reserved for success only.

02 · The signature: concentric radii

Child radius equals parent minus padding.

The one rule everyone steals from Apple and nobody names. Outer container at 20 px with 8 px padding? Inner element at 12 px. The nested corners align visually, which the eye reads as "considered." Break this rule and you get the floating rectangle inside another rectangle look that gives every framework-default UI its amateur aura.

03 · Motion

Spring-smooth, with restraint on the overshoot.

Apple-Craft motion always has a touch of overshoot — it's what makes things feel physical, like objects with mass. But the overshoot is small. Spring-smooth (1.4 peak) is the default; spring-snappy (1.6) on input; spring-bouncy (1.9) only for celebratory moments.

04 · A settings card in Apple-Craft

Glass materials. Tabular nums. A real blue button.

Translucent background (backdrop-filter: blur(20px) saturate(1.2)), SF Pro throughout, tabular numerics on data, concentric radii respected. The sum of these choices is the Apple feeling — there is no single technique.

05 · The trap

Apple-Craft is a system, not a Sketchfab asset.

Copying the visual surface (frosted glass, blue buttons, rounded corners) without the underlying discipline gives you a costume, not a product. Apple-Craft is about what you refuse more than what you add.

Do

Measure nested radii before shipping. If child radius = parent, something is wrong.

Use tabular nums on any column of data. Align decimals. It's a rule, not a suggestion.

Don't

Copy the frosted glass effect without controlling what's behind it.

Use bouncy spring for a dropdown. Reserve it for user-triggered reward moments only.
