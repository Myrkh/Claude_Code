03

Swiss
Quiet.

Grid, Helvetica, black and white and one color. The International Typographic Style applied to screens. Whitespace is a weapon.

A good design is as little design as possible. Dieter Rams · 10 Principles · 1976
Sec. 01

Palette.

Five greys, one black, and a single red. No gradient. No shadow. The discipline is the palette.

Sec. 02

Motion.

Short, standard easing. No spring. Swiss design respects the grid; motion respects the clock.

  • 01 Duration  — 200ms for everything. The only duration. Allowed
  • 02 Easing  — cubic-bezier(0.2, 0, 0, 1). Standard, one only. Allowed
  • 03 Spring  — never used. Springs are a Pacific invention. Banned
  • 04 Micro-interactions  — disabled by default. Enable only for feedback. Banned
  • 05 Decorative animation  — if static communicates it, use static. Banned
Sec. 03

The trap.

Swiss-Quiet fails at the first ornament. Keep the discipline, or you've built a bootstrap admin.

Do

Align everything to the grid. 8pt if you must choose. Every edge aligned is a vote for coherence.

Pick one color and commit. Swiss-Quiet is monochrome plus one accent, not palette exploration.

Don't

Break the grid for creative effect. If the grid is right, it doesn't need breaking.

Use drop shadows to create hierarchy. Use weight, size, and position. Shadows are a crutch.
