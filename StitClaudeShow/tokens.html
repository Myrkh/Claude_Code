The technical spine of Stit'Claude. Every color in OKLCH, every duration named, every easing explicit. Click any token to copy it to your clipboard. This is what theme.css contains, made human.

01

Colors, in OKLCH.

Two scales: ink (neutral warm), coral (accent). Twelve steps each, perceptually uniform. Dark mode shifts the same scale — it doesn't invert it.

OKLCH is a perceptually uniform color space. Two colors with the same L value read as equally bright to the human eye, regardless of their H (hue). That's why every scale step looks correct even when you swap the hue. Hex and HSL can't do this.

Motion has a reason.

Four durations. Seven easings. Every motion in the system cites one of each. Hover any row to preview the curve. Bouncy for reward, snappy for input, smooth for default.

03

Type, in clamps.

A fluid scale from step--2 to step-display. Every size is a clamp() with a minimum, a viewport-fluid middle, and a maximum. Never a pixel value hardcoded.

04

Concentric radii.

Child radius equals parent radius minus its padding. Five steps from sm to pill. Used correctly, a card at 14 px contains items at 10 px, which contain icons at 6 px. Nested alignment, no maths required.

05

Spacing, in fours.

All spacing in multiples of 0.25rem. Ten named values from space-1 (4px) to space-24 (96px). Pixel-precise, token-named. No magic numbers.
