Why hold, not click?

A click is a reflex. A hold is a decision. The two gestures look alike — they produce radically different relationships to the information they reveal.

Stit'Claude · hold
€ 127 400 ▸HOLD600ms

Intentional. You had to decide. A screenshot taken mid-scroll catches only the blur. A colleague glancing over your shoulder catches nothing.

Everyone else · click
€ 127 400

Reflexive. A click is a reflex. Any cursor passing over, any accidental tap, any partial screenshot — all reveal. The gesture carries no weight.

The API

Vanilla. No framework.

A single custom element registers itself on load. Wrap any content, specify a duration, respect reduced motion. The rest is a well-behaved button.

What to avoid

Five ways to break it.

Every signature component has a short list of things that void its purpose. Here's what Reveal-on-Hold refuses to be.

01 · DO NOT
Use it on trivial data.
Reveal-on-Hold exists because the data has weight. A username, a public date, a public status — these must be visible immediately. The gesture loses meaning the moment it's applied to everything.
02 · DO NOT
Set the duration below 400 ms.
Below 400 ms, a hold is indistinguishable from a slow click. The measurability of the gesture is the entire point. 600 ms is the calibrated default. Shorter is a different component.
03 · DO NOT
Persist the revealed state.
A revealed value must re-hide — either on blur, or after a short inactivity window, or on navigation. Persisted reveal defeats the surveillance case for which the component exists.
04 · DO NOT
Omit the reduced-motion fallback.
Users with vestibular or motor conditions must get a single-tap reveal at prefers-reduced-motion: reduce. The privacy benefit is preserved; the effort is reduced.
05 · DO NOT
Stack three reveals in the same viewport.
A page with five Reveal-on-Hold blocks is a page with no signal. If every number requires a gesture, no number is special. One or two per view is the ceiling.
